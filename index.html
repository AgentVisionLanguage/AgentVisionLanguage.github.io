Multimodal Agent AI

Zane Durante1;, Qiuyuan Huang2;, Naoki Wake2;, Ran Gong3;, James Park4, Xiaojian Ma6, Rohan Taori1, Yusuke Noda5, Hoi Vo5, Jianfeng Gao2,
1Stanford University, 2Microsoft Research, Redmond, 3University of California, Los Angeles, 4University of Washington, 5Microsoft Gaming, 6BIGAI,

Abstract

Agent-based multimodal AI systems are becoming a ubiquitous presence in our everyday lives. A promising direction for making these systems more interactive is to embody them as agents within specific environments. The grounding of large foundation models to act as agents within specific environments can provide a way of incorporating visual and contextual information into an embodied system. For example, a system that can perceive user actions, human behavior, environment objects, audio expressions, and the collective sentiment of a scene can be used to inform and direct agent responses within the given environment. We define Multimodal Agent AI as an interactive system that can perceive visual stimuli, language inputs, or other environmentallygrounded data and can produce meaningful actions. In particular, we focus on improving upon agents based on next action predication by incorporating external knowledge, multimodality, and human feedback obtained by the interactive agent. We argue that by developing agentic AI systems in grounded environments, we can also minimize the hallucinations of large foundation models and their ability to generate incorrect outputs. To accelerate research on agent-based multimodal intelligence, we propose a new workshop on multimodal agent AI, which focuses on the broader embodied and agentic aspects of multimodal interactions. In addition, we will organize two challenges and will release two new datasets - CuisineWorld and VideoAnalytica - with a set of baseline models, encouraging researchers across the world to develop new models and systems, and explore ways to evaluate and improve upon performance in our agent-based multimodal leaderboard. To push the frontier of this important area, this workshop and competition aim at bringing researchers and practitioners in the relevant interactive agent AI and multimodal AI fields together, to share ideas and insights. This is an emerging research area that poses new challenges for AI systems and there is still significant room for improvement. A deeper understanding between audio, vision and language has also started to play a key role in human-machine interaction systems. Our workshop will greatly advance large foundation model technologies including visual understanding and large language model generation, generic agent information and human-aesthetic evaluation.
